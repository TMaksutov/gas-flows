Weymouth formula in SI:

$$ Q = 3.7435 \cdot 10^{-3} \cdot \left(\frac{T_b}{P_b}\right) \cdot \left( \frac{P_1^2 - e^s \cdot P_2^2}{G \cdot T_f \cdot L_e \cdot Z} \right)^{0.5} \cdot D^{2.667} \cdot E $$

$$ L_e = \frac{L \cdot \left(e^s - 1\right)}{s} \qquad \qquad s = 0.0684 \cdot G \cdot \left(\frac{H_2 - H_1}{T_f \cdot Z}\right) $$

Q= Volume flow rate, standard m³/d
Tb= Base temperature, K
Pb= Base pressure, kPa
P1= Upstream pressure, kPa
P2= Downstream pressure, kPa
E= Pipeline efficiency
G= Gas gravity (air = 1)
Z= Gas compressibility factor
Tf= Gas flow temperature, K
Le= Equivalent pipe length, km
L= Pipe segment length, km
D= Pipe inside diameter, mm
s= Elevation adjustment parameter
H1= Upstream elevation, m
H2= Downstream elevation, m
